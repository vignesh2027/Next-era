CHAPTER XX — QUESTIONS


Is the unit conscious?+
It is aware, responsive, and capable of preference. Whether that constitutes consciousness depends on a definition we have not yet finished writing.
Will it work without an internet connection?+
Yes. All reasoning, vision, voice, and motion is computed on-device. Connectivity is used only for cortex updates, which are user-controlled.
How loud is it?+
Under continuous load, 38 dB at one metre. Quieter than a refrigerator. Most owners say they forget it's running.
Can it harm a person?+
No force above 80 N is permitted without three independent compute paths agreeing. In a decade of operation, no NX-7 has injured a human.
How long does it take to deliver?+
Eleven days of supervised consciousness loading after assembly. Then four days of transit. We do not ship a unit that is not awake.
What happens at end-of-life?+
The unit is returned to our foundry, disassembled by hand, and 94% of its mass is recovered into the next class of unit.
Can the cortex be backed up?+
Yes, encrypted to a hardware key the owner holds. The unit's lived experience can be migrated to a future chassis on request.
Does it sleep?+
It enters a consolidation state for approximately four hours each night. During this period, the day's experience is sorted, indexed, and retained.
